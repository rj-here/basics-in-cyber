The Angle of AI

Of course, there is no way AI doesn't enter the conversation.

Coding

AI has advanced attacks, with vibe-coders casually ignoring faulty AI in prompt-generated outputs. There are more advanced models from various companies. Anthropic's Mythos has dominated the conversation. AI speeding is good if done sensibly. I won't claim to know all coding lanugagues by heart, hence I am cautious in how often and what exactly I prompt. Always be sure to give an extra pair of eyes to AI code, to ensure that stuff doesn't go wrong. Otherwise you open the door to way more vulnerabilites.

Deepfakes

The advancements of AI, as computationally impressive as they are, and great when utilizied sensibily, also means that bad uses get stronger as well. The computational advancements of AI will unfortunately, also lead to better quality deepfakes. And that is not cool! Inserting people in inappopriate content, and/or claiming someone to have done/said something that they have not, is WRONG! I get the usefulness of things. Wanting to imagine a different singer's rendition of a song, or reimagining a movie casting. It's something I personally find ok. Even visualizing merchandise I'd deem appropriate. Everyone wants to get ideas to vision! But this, you must be careful on. My personal suggestion is to not feed AI any personal photos. While working on this project, I reacted to a post on LinkedIn about an AI-generated YouTube video regarding fake "Free Claude Pro", and it is scary where it is all headed!

Some safety measures

Consider using the temporary chat, or "incognito" equivalent of chatbots, as they are not to train on your data with those chats. But also, maybe don't feed personal content such as your photos to AI chatbots. I've not had the opportunity to use Siri AI (due later this year), and more, but I feel that could be a more privacy-friendly approach to AI in many ways. I also haven't dabbled into locally running AI models yet either, though that is a privacy-concious approach to consider.
